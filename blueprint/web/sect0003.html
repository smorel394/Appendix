\(T\)-acyclic objects

Let \(\mathcal{D},\mathcal{D}'\) be triangulated categories and \(T\colon \mathcal{D}\longrightarrow \mathcal{D}'\) be a triangulated functor. Suppose that we are given a t-structure \((\mathcal{D}^{\leq 0},\mathcal{D}^{\geq 0})\) (resp. \(({\mathcal{D}’}^{\leq 0},{\mathcal{D}’}^{\geq 0})\)) on \(\mathcal{D}\) (resp. \(\mathcal{D}'\)), and denote the cohomology functors of this t-structure by \(\H^ i_t\) (resp. \(\H^ i_{t'}\)) and its heart by \(\mathcal{D}^\heartsuit \) (resp. \({\mathcal{D}’}^\heartsuit \)).

Definition .1

CategoryTheory.Triangulated.TStructure.Acyclic

We say that an object \(X\) of \(\mathcal{D}^\heartsuit \) is \(T\)-acyclic if \(T(X)\) is in \({\mathcal{D}’}^\heartsuit \).

Lemma .2

CategoryTheory.Triangulated.TStructure.isZero_homology_of_acyclic def-acyclic If \(X\) is a \(T\)-acyclic object and \(n\ne 0\) is an integer, then \(\H^ n_{t'}(F(X))\) is zero.

For the proof of Lemma A.1.3(iii), we need a number of facts about preservation of kernels/cokernels/etc. First we prove a statement about preservation of images.

Definition .14

CategoryTheory.Functor.isoImageOfAcyclic def-hTh, def-acyclic If a map \(f\) in \(\mathcal{D}^\heartsuit \) has an acyclic kernel and an acyclic cokernel, then the functor \(T'\) of Definition .7 preserves the image of ‘\(f\).

Remark .15

This isomorphism is not given by CategoryTheory.PreservesImage.iso, because we do not yet know at this point that the kernel and the cokernel of \(f\) are preserved. So we actually construct an image factorization of \(T'(f)\) by hand in imageFactorisation_map_of_acyclic_ker_coker which has the desired image. Then we need to check that the isomorphism of Definition .14 is compatible with Abelian.image.ι and Abelian.factorThruImage, this is done in CategoryTheory.Functor.isoImageOfAcyclic_comp_ι and CategoryTheory.Functor.factorThruImage_comp_isoImageOfAcyclic.

Functor reconstruction

We keep the notation of the previous subsection, and we assume that the t-structure \(t'\) on \(\mathcal{D}'\) is non-degenerate. We also assume that we have filtered triangulated categories \(\DF \) over \(\mathcal{D}\) and \(\DF '\) over \(\mathcal{D}'\), equipped with compatible t-structures \(tF\) and \(tF'\), as well as a filtered triangulated functor \(FT:\DF \to \DF '\) lifing \(T:\mathcal{D}\to \mathcal{D}'\); this is condition (a) of Proposition A.3.2.

Condition (b) of Proposition A.3.2 says that the "obvious" functor from the bounded homotopy category of complexes of \(T\)-acyclic objects in the heart \(\mathcal{D}\) to the derived category of the heart of \(\mathcal{D}\) is a localization functor for the class of morphisms with acyclic cone (i.e. quasi-isomorphisms).

First statement of Proposition A.3.2:

Proposition .1

CategoryTheory.FilteredTriangulated.AcyclicComplexAcyclic_image def-acyclic, def-FilteredLifting, def-compatible

The functor on the bounded homotopy categories induced by the functor \(T''\) of Definition .8 sends a (bounded) exact complex of \(T\)-acyclic objects to an exact complex.

Definition .2

CategoryTheory.FilteredTriangulated.DerivedFunctor prop-AcyclicComplexAcyclic_image

The functor \(DT:\Db (\mathcal{D}^\heartsuit )\to \Db ({\mathcal{D}’}^\heartsuit )\) given by the universal property of the localization functor of condition (b) above, and the property proved in Proposition .1.

Second statement of Proposition A.3.2:

Definition .3

CategoryTheory.FilteredTriangulated.DerivedFunctor_comp def-derived

There is an isomorphism of functors \(\real '\circ DT\stackrel{\sim }{\longrightarrow }T\circ \real \).

D[r, "T“] D’

Remark .4

Suppose that we are in the situation of Proposition A.3.2. If moreover \(T\colon \mathcal{D}\longrightarrow \mathcal{D}'\) is left t-exact and if \(\mathcal{I}\) is cogenerating in \(\mathcal{C}\) (i.e. every object of \(\mathcal{C}\) has a monomorphism into an object of \(\mathcal{I}\)), then the functor \(\H^ 0(T)\colon \mathcal{C}\longrightarrow \mathcal{C}'\) admits a right derived functor \(RT\colon \Dpl (\mathcal{C})\longrightarrow \Dpl (\mathcal{C}')\) by Proposition 13.3.5 of  [ 3 ] , and the construction of \(RT\) in that proposition shows that \(RT\) sends that \(\Db (\mathcal{C})\) to \(\Db (\mathcal{C}')\) and that \(DT\) is the restriction of \(RT\) to \(\Db (\mathcal{C})\). We have a similar statement if \(T\) is right t-exact and \(\mathcal{I}\) is generating in \(\mathcal{C}\).

Remark .5

By Proposition 10.2.7 of  [ 3 ] , to check assumption (b) of Proposition A.3.2, it suffices to find triangulated subcategories \(\mathcal{D}_0=\Kb (\mathcal{C})\supset \mathcal{D}_1\supset \ldots \supset \mathcal{D}_r=\Kb (\mathcal{I})\) of \(\Kb (\mathcal{C})\) such that, for every \(i\in \{ 1,\ldots ,r-1\} \), one of the following conditions holds:

  • For every \(X\in \Ob \mathcal{D}_i\), there exists a quasi-isomorphism \(X\longrightarrow Y\) with \(Y\in \Ob \mathcal{D}_{i+1}\).

  • For every \(X\in \Ob \mathcal{D}_i\), there exists a quasi-isomorphism \(Y\longrightarrow X\) with \(Y\in \Ob \mathcal{D}_{i+1}\).
